## Waiata Puāwai (session flow)
A simple outline of how our hui/practice time often runs. (This can be adapted depending on the kaupapa for the day.)

1. **Karakia tīmatanga** — opening karakia
2. **Recitement of acknowledgement** — acknowledgements
3. **Waiata hīmene** — hymn
4. **Whaikōrero** — speech of welcome / kōrero
5. **Waiata tautoko** — supporting waiata for speaker
6. **Whakawhanaungatanga / Pepeha** — new whānau connections
7. **Whakahā** — breathing exercises
8. **Ngā waiata** — songs (learning + practice)
9. **Pānui / Poroporoaki** — notices / farewell
10. **Karakia whakamutunga** — closing karakia

## Pepeha (template)
Mix and match what fits for you. It’s okay to keep it simple.

- **Tēnā koutou katoa**
- **Ko _ te maunga** — is the mountain
- **Ko _ te awa / roto** — is the river / lake
- **Ko _ te waka** — is the waka
- **Ko _ te whenua tupu** — is where I grew up
- **Ko _ te kāinga** — is my home
- **Nō _ au** — I’m from
- **Ko _ te marae** — is the marae
- **Kei _ au e noho ana** — I am living in
- **He _ au i _** — I am a _ at _

## Whakahā (breathing)
A short breathing moment to settle, warm up, and get our voices ready.

- Take 2–3 slow breaths together.
- Relax shoulders/jaw.
- Gentle hum into the first waiata.
