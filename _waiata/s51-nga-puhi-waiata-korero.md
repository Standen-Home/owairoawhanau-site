## Meaning
to the voice that is crying out
and see pouring out
the tears.
But as the sun shines
the pain disappears and
the sadness falls way
for this is another day
Rise up all of you
and hold firmly and securely and forever
to the great teachings
As you travel the world
that we are people who stand proud
we, the descendants of those who have been
lost in the night.
This is the thanks we give to you
To our aunts and uncles,
mothers and fathers
Here we are, the fruit of your labour
This was written by school teacher P
He wrote it in 1983 whist he was teaching at Wainuiomata Intermediate, and his class "Te Kahui Tamariki" were the first students to perform this waiata, which was at the time referred to as "Whakarongo Mai".
This song was his way of expressing his view that Nga
uhi whanau should stand up and fight for the right to speak the language of their ancestors.
It was first recorded in 1994. Piripi died in 2001.
