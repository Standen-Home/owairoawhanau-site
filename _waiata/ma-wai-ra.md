## Kupu
<pre class="lyrics">
Ma wai ra e taurima
te marae i waho nei?
Ma te tika
ma te pono
me te aroha e.

  Noho au i tōku whare
  whakarongo ki te tangi
  o te manu
  rere i runga
  rere I waho e

  Ngā wawata, nga roimata
  i maringi ki te pa e
  he ara hei huarahi
  Mo te aroha
  Tangihia au tinei
  kua riro koutou;
  ki te iwi
  ki te rangi
  ki te Atua e.

Ma wai ra e taurima
te marae i waho nei?
Ma te tika
ma te pono
me te aroha e.
</pre>

## Notes
- Ō Wairoa Marae Waiata sing through the first verse twice
- A Ngati Porou lament sung at tangi. The Ma Wai Ra verse is also sung at policy-making hui by the hosts, as a reminder to put personal motives aside.
